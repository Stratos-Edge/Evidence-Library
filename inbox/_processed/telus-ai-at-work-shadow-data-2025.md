## Verified findings (quote-faithful to the release)

- **57%** of enterprise employees who use GenAI at work admit to entering **sensitive/confidential information** into publicly available GenAI assistants (ChatGPT, Microsoft Copilot, Google Gemini).
- **68%** access public GenAI assistants through **personal accounts** rather than company-approved tools.
- Types of data entered: personal data 31%; product/project details incl. unreleased prototypes 29%; customer information 21%; confidential company financials 11%.
- **22%** of employees who already have a company-provided GenAI assistant still use personal GenAI accounts.
- Policy/training gaps: only 24% require mandatory AI training; 44% have no AI policy or don't know if one exists; 50% unsure whether they comply; 42% say there are no repercussions for non-compliance; 29% acknowledge a policy prohibiting sensitive-data entry (which they enter anyway).
- Why they use it: 60% work faster, 57% job easier, 49% better performance; 84% want to keep using AI at work.
