## Verified findings (quote-faithful to the release)

- **70%** of hospital leaders reported **at least one AI pilot that never scaled** beyond a limited deployment — most often due to weak or poorly chosen endpoints, data gaps, or misalignment with real-world clinical workflows.
- **80%** said AI vendor claims are difficult to verify without a formal AI governance program.
- Health systems with an **AI Governance Council are more than 2× as likely to achieve a positive ROI on AI investments within 12 months** of a pilot, vs. those without such a structure.
- Programs with dashboards, clear ownership, and structured reporting reach early ROI in **~7.5 months on average vs. ~13.5 months** in less structured environments.
- **71%** say health equity is "planned" in AI initiatives but not consistently measured during pilots.
- ~6 in 10 AI contracts require no formal re-validation when a model is materially updated (silent performance-drift exposure).
- Shadow-mode pilots run 4–8 weeks were significantly more likely to advance without safety concerns; written gate criteria correlated with fewer pilot extensions and faster decisions.

> "AI governance is not a brake on innovation, it's the steering wheel." — Doug Brown, Founder, Black Book Research
